FILIPINO PORK ADOBO

Description

Adobo refers to a method of marinating and stewing for any cut of meat or fish in a briny mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, and spices. Filipino adobo should not be confused with the spicy Spanish adobo sauce. Although they both share the Spanish name, they are vastly different in flavor and ingredients.


Pork Adobo Cooking Procedure

  1. Get a pot and make it nice and hot over medium heat. Pour some oil and sauté garlic and onions. Add pork and continue sautéing until cooked.
  2. Put 1 tsp of oyster sauce and simmer then add water. Let boil. Cover and cook in low heat for 20-30 minutes or until the pork gets tender. Add more beef broth or water if the liquid starts to dry quickly.
  3. Pour-in the vinegar. Let the liquid re-boil. Stir and cook for 8 minutes.
  4. Taste your pork adobo and decide to add salt if needed.
  5. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve.
